Protect Now

take steps to protect yourself

Wear A Face Mask

Clean your hands before you put your mask on, as well as before and after you take it off, and after you touch it at any time. Make sure it covers both your nose, mouth and chin.!

learn more

Wash Your Hands

You can help yourself and your loved ones stay healthy by washing your hands often, especially during these key times when you are likely to get and spread germs: 1.Before, during, and after preparing food 2.Before and after eating food 3.Before and after caring for someone at home who is sick with vomiting or diarrhea 4.Before and after treating a cut or wound 5.After using the toilet 6.After changing diapers or cleaning up a child who has used the toilet 7.After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing 8.After touching an animal, animal feed, or animal waste 9.After handling pet food or pet treats 10.After touching garbage!

learn more

Avoid Close Contact

Close contact is defined as being within 2 meters or 6 feet from a person infected with COVID-19 for a prolonged time. This includes living with, visiting, or sharing a closed space with the infected person. Even being coughed upon by a person with COVID-9 infection can qualify you as a close contact.!

things not to do during covid

  • Do Not Share Eating
  • Do Not Touch Your Face or Nose
  • Do Not Contact Sick People
  • Wash their hands frequently using an alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water.
  • Cover their mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.
  • Avoid close contact with anyone who has a fever and cough.
  • Seek medical care early if they have fever, cough and difficulty breathing.
  • Share previous travel history with their healthcare provider.
